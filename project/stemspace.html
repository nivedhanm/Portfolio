STEM Space

   Lean Startup    Product Design    Mobile    User Research    Business Model

STEM Space is the project that is closest to my heart. It grew my interest towards UX and product creation. I conceived the idea for this at an Aggies Invent(48-hour innovation competition). The prompt was to interest children in STEM education. I further took this product to Startup Aggieland and tested it through the lean startup methodology and thus understanding more about the entrepreneurial process.

The development of this process has gone through several iterations, from the initial prototype (seen above) which simply focused on science experiments and gamification to interest students to understanding the true customer problem through the lean startup process. I interviewed 100 potential customers, ranging from teachers, Parents and even children to truly begin to understand the magnitude of the problem. I paused work on the project for a few months to gain a deeper understanding of the problem and reflect on why I got the feeling that I was missing something key. During this I reframed the problem and sought to find how educators are approaching this problem now and I came across Project Based Learning conepts. Gathering this information I re-brainstromed to assess what the real value of the product is.

The product now involves a multi-sided market of both teachers, students and even involving the parents to an extent. The idea stands on three aspects

  1. A Social Network platform with gamification elements to engage students
  2. A content platform to create and share content among teachers
  3. An Assessment platform which leverages formative assessment instead of summative assessment

The essense here is to digitize Project Based Learning. focus formative assessment through reflections and content focusing on key knowledge and success skills. The social and gamification features exists solely to sustain interest and build on student enquiry.
